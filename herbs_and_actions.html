Herbs and Actions

Bai He (Lily Bulb): Used for clearing heat, moistening the lungs, and stopping coughs. Also beneficial for calming the spirit.

Jin Yin Hua (Honeysuckle Flower): Clears heat and relieves toxicity, especially useful for fever, sore throat, and skin infections.

Shi Gao (Gypsum): Strongly clears heat and drains fire, often used for high fever, irritability, and thirst.

Gan Jiang (Dried Ginger Root): Warms the middle burner, expels cold, and restores devastated yang. Used for cold limbs, nausea, and diarrhea.

Rou Gui (Cinnamon Bark): Warms the kidneys, strengthens yang, and disperses cold. Useful for abdominal pain due to cold, lower back pain, and frequent urination.

Wu Zhu Yu (Evodia Fruit): Warms the middle and lower burners, disperses cold, and alleviates pain. Commonly used for headaches, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Ren Shen (Ginseng Root): Strongly tonifies the primal qi of the five organs, calms the spirit, and stops thirst. Used for extreme collapse of qi, shortness of breath, and weak pulse.

Huang Qi (Astragalus Root): Tonifies qi and blood, raises yang, and boosts the immune system. Helps with fatigue, loss of appetite, and spontaneous sweating.

Dang Gui (Angelica Sinensis): Tonifies and invigorates blood, regulates menstruation, and alleviates pain. Used for blood deficiency, menstrual disorders, and pain due to blood stasis.

Huang Qin (Baical Skullcap Root): Clears heat and dries dampness, detoxifies, and stops bleeding. Used for high fever, cough, and diarrhea.

Jin Yin Hua (Honeysuckle Flower): Clears heat and relieves toxicity, particularly effective for sore throat, skin infections, and boils.

Lian Qiao (Forsythia Fruit): Clears heat and toxins, dissipates nodules, and expels pus. Commonly used for fever, carbuncles, and sores.

Gou Qi Zi (Goji Berries): Nourishes and tonifies liver and kidney, benefits the essence, and brightens the eyes. Used for dizziness, blurred vision, and fatigue.

Mai Men Dong (Ophiopogon Root): Nourishes yin, moistens the lungs, and clears the heart. Helps with dry cough, thirst, and constipation.

Shu Di Huang (Prepared Rehmannia Root): Tonifies blood and yin, nourishes essence, and fills the marrow. Used for dizziness, tinnitus, and weak lower back.
